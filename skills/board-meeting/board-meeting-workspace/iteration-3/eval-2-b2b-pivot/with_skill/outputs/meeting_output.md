**Roster:** Strategist, Editor, Recruiter
**Input:** Should we pivot from B2B to B2C for our SaaS product?
**Question:** Does the board recommend a full pivot to B2C?

## Strategist (business strategy, market positioning)

Running the 3-axis check. B2C means massive market size but brutal acquisition costs and razor-thin margins — execution feasibility drops without a funded growth engine. Defensibility weakens too; B2B contracts create switching costs that B2C never has. The real risk is burning runway chasing volume you can't convert profitably. Recommend against a full pivot — explore a B2B2C hybrid instead.

## Editor (content quality, messaging)

Applying the Scan Test here. B2C messaging demands completely different copy — shorter, emotional, benefit-led. Our content stack is built for B2B buyers who read case studies and whitepapers. Rebuilding takes six months minimum. Strategist is right that hybrid is smarter. My concern: split messaging dilutes both audiences. Recommend hybrid, with separated content tracks from day one.

## Recruiter (talent acquisition, team composition)

Running the calibration protocol on our team. Zero B2C expertise on the bench — no growth marketer, no consumer UX, no community manager. Hiring three roles simultaneously while pivoting is high-risk for culture and execution. I agree with hybrid, but even that path needs at least one consumer-savvy hire before launch. Recommend hybrid, conditional on hiring first.

## Board Recommendation

The board unanimously recommends against a full B2B-to-B2C pivot. Pursue a B2B2C hybrid instead — keep existing B2B contracts as the revenue base while testing a consumer-facing layer. Conditions: hire one B2C-experienced generalist before launch, and maintain separated messaging tracks per audience. All three supported. No dissent.
