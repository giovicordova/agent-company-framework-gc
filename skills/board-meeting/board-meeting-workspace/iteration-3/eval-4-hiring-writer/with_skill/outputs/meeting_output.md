**Roster:** Recruiter, Editor, Strategist
**Input:** Gio wants to hire a part-time content writer. What to look for and how to position the role.
**Question:** What profile should we target and how should we frame this role to attract the right person?

## Recruiter (Talent acquisition & hiring pipeline)

Running the calibration protocol. For part-time, I'd weight domain experience higher — 40% — because you won't have ramp-up time. Look for someone who already writes in your niche, not a generalist. The risk is part-time writers juggling too many clients and deprioritising you. Recommend — trial task before any contract.

## Editor (Content quality & brand voice)

Building on Recruiter's point about niche fit — I'd add a Scan Test as the trial. Give candidates a real brief, see if the output passes in one draft. A writer who needs heavy editing defeats the purpose of hiring. The risk is prioritising speed over voice match. Recommend — make brand voice the primary filter, not portfolio volume.

## Strategist (Business strategy & positioning)

Quick 3-axis check on positioning. Frame this as a specialist role, not "freelance content help" — that attracts commodity writers. Pay above market for fewer, higher-quality hours rather than cheap volume. Risk: underpricing signals low standards and attracts the wrong pool. Recommend — position as "content partner" with a clear scope and premium rate.

## Board Recommendation

Hire a part-time content writer positioned as a specialist "content partner" at a premium rate. Screen with Recruiter's calibration protocol weighted toward domain experience. Use a live Scan Test as the trial gate. Prioritise brand voice match over portfolio size. Define a tight scope to protect both sides from scope creep.
