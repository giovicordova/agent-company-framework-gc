# Board Meeting — Paid Newsletter for Audio Engineers x AI Tools

**Date:** 2026-04-12
**Called by:** Gio
**Agenda:** Should we launch a paid newsletter targeting audio engineers who want to learn AI tools?

---

## Strategist

Running the 3-axis check on this.

**Market size — Medium.** The audio engineering market is niche but passionate. There are roughly 100,000+ active audio professionals globally who follow online education (Sound on Sound, Puremix, Pensado's Place audiences). The subset interested in AI is growing fast but still early. This is not a mass-market play. You are looking at maybe 5,000–15,000 addressable subscribers within the first two years.

**Execution feasibility — High.** You have ten years of audio credibility and you are already building with AI daily. You are not theorising — you are the use case. A newsletter is low-overhead: no inventory, no employees, no complex tech stack. Substack or Beehiiv gets you live in a day.

**Defensibility — This is the weak point.** A newsletter is easy to copy. Anyone with audio credentials and AI curiosity can launch the same thing next month. Your moat is not the format — it is your specific angle: you are an audio engineer who actually ships software with AI, not someone writing about AI from the sidelines. That lived experience is the defensible edge. Lean into it hard.

**Revenue model reality check:** At $10/month, you need 500 paying subscribers to hit $5,000/month. That is achievable but not trivial in a niche market. The real play is not the newsletter revenue itself — it is the strategic flywheel. The newsletter builds an audience, the audience feeds your other projects (tools, consulting, courses), and those projects feed newsletter content. The newsletter is the top of the funnel, not the product.

**Recommendation:** Go, but do not treat this as a standalone business. Treat it as the audience-building engine for a broader ecosystem. Launch free first, build to 1,000+ subscribers, then gate premium content. Do not charge on day one — you have no proven demand yet.

---

## Editor

The concept passes the Scan Test on positioning: "audio engineer who builds with AI, teaching other audio engineers to do the same." That is clear. A reader gets it in three seconds.

Where this will succeed or fail is in the content itself. The audio engineering world is drowning in generic AI hype pieces — "AI will change music production!" — that say nothing useful. If your newsletter reads like that, it is dead on arrival.

What works: practical, specific, from-the-trenches content.

- "I used Claude to build a gain-staging calculator this week — here is what worked and what did not"
- "Three AI tools I tested for stem separation — honest results with audio examples"
- "How I automated my mastering chain intake with a simple script"

What kills it: thought-leadership fluff, trend roundups with no original insight, anything that could have been written by someone who has never touched a fader.

**Content cadence matters.** Weekly is the right frequency for paid. Biweekly loses momentum. Daily is unsustainable for one person writing quality material.

**Brand voice recommendation:** Write like you talk in the studio — direct, practical, no nonsense. The audio world respects craft and hates pretension. Your voice should sound like a senior engineer explaining something to a colleague, not a marketer selling a vision.

**Recommendation:** The concept is sound editorially. The key risk is content discipline — keeping every issue practical and earned rather than sliding into commentary. I would draft three sample issues before launch to pressure-test whether you can sustain the quality and voice at weekly cadence.

## Recruiter

Running the calibration protocol on this — but instead of a candidate, I am assessing you against the role of "newsletter creator and operator."

**Technical skills (40%):** Strong. You have genuine audio engineering depth and daily AI building experience. You are not pretending to know either domain.

**Domain experience (30%):** Strong on audio, developing on AI. The gap is in content creation and audience building specifically. You have not run a newsletter before. That is a learnable skill, but it is a skill — writing consistently, managing subscriber expectations, handling the marketing side.

**Culture alignment (20%):** High. The audio engineering community values authenticity and craft. You come from that world. You will not have a credibility problem.

**Growth potential (10%):** High. This positions you at the intersection of two growing fields.

**The human element the Strategist is not accounting for:** Running a weekly newsletter is a commitment. It is not just writing — it is the pressure of a recurring deadline, subscriber expectations, and the emotional weight of publishing publicly in a community you belong to. Before launching, be honest about whether this energises you or feels like a chore. If it is a chore, it will show in the writing within two months and subscribers will leave.

**Skill gap flag:** You will need to learn audience growth tactics — SEO for newsletters, social distribution, community building. These are not audio skills or AI skills. Consider whether you want to learn these yourself or eventually bring someone in.

**Recommendation:** You are a strong fit for this role, but I would suggest a 4-week trial run. Write four issues, send them to a small group (20-30 people from your network), and gauge the response and your own energy levels. If both are positive, scale up. If the writing feels forced, rethink the format — maybe a podcast or video series suits you better.
