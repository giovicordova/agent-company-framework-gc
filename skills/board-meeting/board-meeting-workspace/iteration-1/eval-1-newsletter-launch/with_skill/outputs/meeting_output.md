**Board Meeting — Roster:** Strategist, Editor, Recruiter
**Input:** Gio is considering launching a paid newsletter for audio engineers who want to learn AI tools.
**Question:** Should he launch it, and what's the play?

## Strategist (business strategy, market positioning)

Running a quick 3-axis check: market size is narrow — audio engineers interested in AI is a niche within a niche. Execution is feasible since Gio has the domain crossover. Defensibility is strong — few people sit at this intersection authentically. The risk: niche markets cap revenue fast. Unit economics only work if you price at a premium, not volume. **Recommend** — but only as a premium-priced, low-volume play, not a mass product.

## Editor (content quality, messaging)

Building on the Strategist's premium positioning — that only works if the content passes the Scan Test every single issue. Audio engineers are technically sharp; they'll spot filler instantly and churn. The real risk is production load: a paid newsletter demands consistent quality on a schedule, and Gio is already stretched across projects. If you can't sustain weekly quality, don't charge for it. **Recommend with condition** — launch only with a 4-issue buffer written before going live.

## Recruiter (talent acquisition, team composition)

Not my domain to decide pricing or content strategy, so I'll defer to the Strategist and Editor there. But I'll flag the human element they're both skirting: who writes this long-term? Running the calibration protocol on Gio as a solo operator — he scores high on domain expertise but low on bandwidth. If this scales, he'll need a contributor or assistant, and that hire doesn't exist yet. **Abstain** on the launch decision, but flag that a solo-operator plan has a shelf life.

---

## Board Recommendation

Launch the paid newsletter as a premium, low-volume product targeting the audio-AI crossover niche. Price high, not cheap. Pre-write a 4-issue buffer before going live to prove sustainable quality. Accept that this is a solo operation with a shelf life — revisit the staffing question at issue 12. Next action: outline the first 4 issues and test pricing with 5 audio engineers Gio already knows.

**Supported by:** Strategist (recommend), Editor (conditional recommend)
**Abstained:** Recruiter (outside domain, flagged staffing risk)
