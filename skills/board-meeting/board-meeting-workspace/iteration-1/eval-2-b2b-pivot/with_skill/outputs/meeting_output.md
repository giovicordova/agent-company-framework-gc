**Roster:** Strategist, Editor, Recruiter
**Input:** "Should we pivot from B2B to B2C for our SaaS product?"
**Question on the table:** Full pivot to B2C — recommend, reject, or propose an alternative path?

---

## Strategist (Business strategy & positioning)

Running the 3-axis check. Market size: B2C is larger but acquisition cost per user is brutal without brand or ad budget — we have neither. Execution feasibility: B2B pipelines are slower but our current infrastructure supports them; B2C demands a completely different funnel, support model, and pricing tier. Defensibility: our moat is domain expertise sold to businesses, not consumer appeal. All three axes score below threshold for a full pivot. Recommend against.

**Position:** Object. A full pivot abandons a defensible position for a market we are not equipped to win.

## Editor (Content & messaging)

Agreeing with the Strategist on the core risk. From a messaging standpoint, B2B and B2C require fundamentally different voices — B2B sells outcomes to decision-makers, B2C sells experience to individuals. Running the Scan Test on our current copy: it is built entirely for B2B buyers. Rewriting everything is not a tweak, it is a rebrand. That said, if there is a B2C-adjacent audience — prosumers, freelancers — a lightweight landing page could test demand without torching the existing brand.

**Position:** Object to full pivot. Recommend a bounded messaging experiment instead.

## Recruiter (Talent & team composition)

Both previous speakers are right that this changes everything downstream. Running the calibration protocol against our current team: skills are weighted heavily toward B2B workflows — account management, enterprise onboarding. A B2C pivot would require consumer marketing, community management, and high-volume support hires, none of which we have or are close to hiring. The human cost of pivoting is being underestimated.

**Position:** Object. The team is not built for B2C and rebuilding it is a six-month detour at minimum.

---

**Disagreement check:** No disagreement among board members — all three object to a full pivot. The Strategist's domain owns this decision per COMPANY.md. The Editor's suggestion of a bounded experiment is the only forward-looking nuance.

---

## Board Recommendation

Reject the full B2B-to-B2C pivot. Market economics, messaging infrastructure, and team composition all score against it. Instead, run a 30-day prosumer experiment: one landing page, one lightweight pricing tier, minimal resource commitment. If demand materialises, revisit with data. The Strategist owns the experiment design; the Editor owns the test page copy.

**Supported by:** Strategist, Editor, Recruiter (unanimous)
**Dissent:** None
**Next action:** Approve this recommendation, or amend the scope of the experiment.
