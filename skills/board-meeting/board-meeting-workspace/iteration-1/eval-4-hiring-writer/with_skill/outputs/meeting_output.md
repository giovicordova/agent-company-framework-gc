## Board Meeting

**Roster:** Recruiter, Editor, Strategist
**Input:** "We need to hire a part-time content writer. What should we look for and how should we position the role?"
**Question:** What profile to target and how to position a part-time content writer role.

---

## Recruiter (Talent acquisition & hiring pipeline)

This is squarely my domain. Running the calibration protocol with adjusted weights for a part-time creative role: domain experience 40%, writing craft 30%, culture alignment 20%, growth potential 10%. Look for someone with a portfolio showing range across formats — not just blog posts. Part-time positioning matters: frame it as a specialist engagement, not a junior slot. The risk is attracting generalists who churn shallow content. Screen with a paid trial piece, not interviews alone. **Recommend proceeding.**

## Editor (Content quality & brand voice)

Recruiter's calibration protocol is sound, but I'd push writing craft higher than 30% — that's the whole point of the hire. The Scan Test needs to be part of screening: give candidates a real brief, see if their output passes in one read. The risk I see is hiring someone whose prose needs heavy editing, which defeats the purpose. Require a portfolio piece that shows they can match an existing voice, not just write in their own. **Recommend, with the Scan Test built into screening.**

## Strategist (Business strategy & positioning)

Quick 3-axis check: market size — large freelance writer pool, easy to source. Execution feasibility — part-time roles attract better talent if positioned as ongoing retainer rather than gig work. Defensibility — low, but that's fine for this function. I'd add: define output expectations in hours-per-week and pieces-per-month upfront, not vaguely. The risk is scope creep — part-time becomes full-time cost without full-time commitment. Cap the engagement with a 90-day review clause. **Recommend with defined scope boundaries.**

---

## Board Recommendation

Proceed with the hire. Position it as a specialist retainer (not a gig) with defined weekly hours and monthly output targets. Screen using a paid trial piece evaluated against the Scan Test and the Recruiter's calibration protocol with writing craft weighted at 35%+. Include a 90-day review clause to prevent scope creep. All three board members recommend.
