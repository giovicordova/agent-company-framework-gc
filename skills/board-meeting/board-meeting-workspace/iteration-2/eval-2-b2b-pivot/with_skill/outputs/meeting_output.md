## Strategist (Business strategy, market positioning, revenue)

Running the 3-axis check. B2C market size is massive but acquisition costs are brutal — you're competing with funded incumbents for every eyeball. B2B gives you smaller pools but higher LTV and stickier contracts. Defensibility drops in B2C unless you have a network effect or brand moat. Execution feasibility is low without a consumer marketing engine. Recommend against a full pivot.

## Editor (Content quality, messaging, brand voice)

Agreeing with Strategist on the core risk. B2C messaging demands a completely different voice — emotional, benefit-led, high-frequency content cycles. Your current brand assets and copy framework are built for B2B buyers who scan for ROI. Rebuilding that from scratch is a six-month project minimum. The Scan Test says: your messaging won't land with consumers as-is. Recommend against.

## Recruiter (Talent acquisition, team composition, hiring)

Running the calibration protocol on the team gap. A B2C pivot requires consumer marketing, community management, and support at scale — roles you don't have and aren't hiring for. Current team composition scores well against B2B demands but poorly against B2C requirements. Hiring that bench takes months and burns runway. Not my call on strategy, but the people gap is real. Recommend against.

## Board Recommendation

The board unanimously recommends against a full B2B-to-B2C pivot. Acquisition costs, messaging rebuild, and talent gaps make it high-risk with no clear advantage over your current position. If consumer reach is the goal, explore a B2B2C hybrid — sell to businesses who serve consumers — preserving your existing strengths while expanding reach. Concrete next step: validate whether any current B2B clients have consumer-facing distribution you could partner on.
