**Roster:** Recruiter, Editor, Strategist
**Input:** Gio wants to hire a part-time content writer. What to look for and how to position the role.
**Question:** What profile should we target and how should we frame the role to attract the right person?

## Recruiter (Talent Acquisition)

Running the calibration protocol with adjusted weights: domain experience 40%, writing craft 30%, culture alignment 20%, growth potential 10%. For part-time, prioritise someone already producing content independently — freelancers or portfolio creators who want steady anchor income. The risk is treating this as a junior hire; part-time attracts senior people if positioned as creative autonomy, not task execution. Recommend — frame it as a retained creative partner, not an employee.

## Editor (Content Quality)

Building on Recruiter's point about craft: the screening must include a Scan Test. Give candidates a real brief, evaluate whether their draft passes in five seconds. Portfolio alone misleads — edited samples hide weak first-draft instincts. The risk is hiring someone whose prose needs heavy editing, which defeats the point of part-time. Recommend — live writing test, non-negotiable.

## Strategist (Business Strategy)

Quick 3-axis check. Market: plenty of writers available, buyer's market — good. Execution: part-time works only if scope is pre-defined; vague briefs create scope creep that costs more than full-time. Defensibility: a writer who learns your voice becomes a moat. Risk is rotating freelancers who never compound brand knowledge. Recommend — fixed deliverables per week, three-month trial, then evaluate retention.

## Board Recommendation

Hire a part-time content writer positioned as a retained creative partner with defined weekly deliverables. Screen using the calibration protocol with writing craft weighted heavily, and require a live Scan Test as a gate. Structure as a three-month trial with fixed scope to prevent creep and assess voice fit. All supported, no dissent.
